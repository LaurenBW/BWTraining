BruntWork · RISE Framework · Activity 1 — Relationships

Stakeholder Map

Every client account has multiple stakeholders — and each one needs a different conversation. This activity practises identifying who you're working with, what each person actually needs, and how to plan your relationship-building deliberately rather than by instinct.

Case study
Brightside Legal — Melbourne law firm. 3 BruntWork staff (Priya Nair: paralegal VA, Marcus Obi: bookkeeper, Lena Park: admin assistant). Part-time contract, 20 hrs/week each. Account is now 10 weeks old. CSM: Alex Rivera, AU/NZ shift.

Part 1 — Worked Example

Below is a completed stakeholder map for Brightside Legal at Week 10. Read through it carefully before completing your own version. Pay attention to why each relationship quality score has been given — not just the number.

Highly engaged, zero support queries since Week 1, praised by James. Stable and performing. Send a brief "checking in" message this week — not about issues, just to acknowledge strong performance. Keeps the relationship warm and gives her a moment to flag anything quietly.
Marcus Obi Bookkeeper (BW staff) User Clear payroll and invoice processes, consistent information, not being caught between client and BruntWork on financial questions. Level 2. Consistent attendance, no major issues raised. Less visible than Priya — worth a direct check-in to confirm all is well. Send a direct check-in message to Marcus this week. Ask if he has everything he needs from a process standpoint. Low effort, high signal value.
What makes this good: Each stakeholder has a persona label, a relationship level (not just a score) with a specific reason, and a next action that is concrete and time-bound. The actions are value-first — they offer something or gather something useful, rather than just "touching base."
⚠ The quiet client signal — Brightside Legal context

Sarah has not initiated any contact in 10 weeks. She responds when contacted but has never reached out proactively. At BruntWork, because clients cannot see their staff day-to-day, this silence is a risk signal — not a green light. The QBR at Week 10 is your opportunity to move her from Level 1–2 to Level 2–3. If you don't, she enters renewal without genuine trust in the partnership.

Reflection questions

1. Which stakeholder relationship needs the most work right now — and what is the one thing that would make the biggest difference to it?

2. Are you having the right conversation with each persona? Or are you using the same message for everyone?

3. Do you have a quiet client in this account — someone who hasn't initiated contact in a while? What does that silence tell you?

4. Who is your internal champion — the person most likely to help you influence the others? How are you using that relationship?

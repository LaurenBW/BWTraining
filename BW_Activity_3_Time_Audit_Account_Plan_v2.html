Time Audit + RISE Account Plan

Strategy starts with honesty about where your time actually goes — and what you want to achieve with each account. This activity has three parts: a time audit, a RISE account plan for Brightside Legal, and a growth conversation planner for the part-time to full-time conversion.

Case study
Brightside Legal — Week 10. 3 BW staff on part-time contracts (20 hrs/week each). Account performing well — 96%+ attendance across 3 months, portal usage growing, one overdue invoice resolved. James mentioned in Week 6 that Sarah is considering a 4th hire next quarter. The QBR is this week. CSM: Alex Rivera, 18 accounts, AU/NZ shift.

When to raise it

Raise after presenting the 3-month data narrative — when the performance evidence is freshest in the room. Do not raise it at the start of the QBR (too transactional) or at the very end (too rushed). Aim for the "After" section of the Before/During/After structure — when you're talking about what the next quarter looks like.

How to frame it

Suggested framing for Alex: "The team has been performing really well over the past three months — 96% attendance, platform adoption growing consistently. Many of our clients find that once the team is settled and delivering at this level, full-time makes more commercial sense than part-time — you're getting the full benefit of the relationship without the limitations of the hours cap. It's also usually more cost-effective per hour at full-time rates. Is that something worth exploring for any of the three roles?"

Objections to prepare for

ObjectionPrepared response
"We didn't budget for that this quarter.""Completely understand — it doesn't need to happen immediately. It might be worth flagging to Sarah as something to factor into next quarter's planning, especially given the performance data we've just reviewed."
"Let's see how the next few months go first.""That's a fair approach. What would you need to see over the next 60 days that would make you feel confident about making that call?"
"What's the difference in cost?""I can get you a clear breakdown from our Finance team — I'll include it in the follow-up email after today's call."

Next step if they engage

If Sarah or James shows genuine interest: "Great — I'll put together a simple overview of what full-time looks like for each of the three roles and what the commercial difference would be. I'll have that to you by [date]. Does that work?" Then do it within 48 hours.

What makes this good: The timing is deliberate — not improvised. The framing leads with value ("more commercial sense") not with upsell language. The objections are prepared in advance with specific, non-pushy responses. And the next step is concrete and time-bound.
